Dealing with 4th year at University
Connor
January 30th 2020

Coming from somebody who has now been in education for 6 years, Started this as an Introduction to Digital Media student with not having an incline as to what I wanted to do with my life, to now being in my 4th year at Edinburgh Napier University for Web Design and Development. I am afraid to say I’m not sure anyone or anything can prepare you for fourth year at university, the constant deadlines, the never-ending reading and the constant feeling of doom. That isn’t to say fourth year is all negative however my fourth year is one of special terms, from lecturers being on strike to a global pandemic, my fourth-year experience I believe will go down in history as being an eventful one. Even to the extent that my grandchildren may learn about this in school as the era of toilet roll hoarding. However, not the point we are here for.

The major thing to take away from any of this is, listen to your supervisors and lecturers! What they tell you in your first- and second-year lectures will be handy when it comes to dissertation time, take it on board. The more prepared you are, the better you are going to feel when it comes to writing that dreaded dissertation. Honestly, although stressful, no one cannot say it isn’t stressful, you will get through this and you are not alone, writers block is real but move away from your desk, your study area, take a breather then move back with an open mind. Trust me, you will need to do this plenty in your last year of university. Bear in mind that you are not alone in this, there are countless other students who are considering making this their gap year and thinking of taking that dream holiday that no student can afford, because face it, we are students. I guess the main message to take away from here is, you can do this.
