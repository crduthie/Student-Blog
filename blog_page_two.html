Making the big move to another city
Connor
February 1st 2020

Throwing it back to the first post made on this blog, I mentioned originally being from Aberdeen, the Granite city, which as the name implies was completely covered in grey. This was one of the reasons why I moved, who wants to spend the rest of their lives staring at grey. All wasn’t bad, my family all still stay there, and visiting is one of the best feelings but that isn’t what we are here to discuss. MY BIG MOVE. So, back in september 2018 I made the move from Aberdeen to Edinburgh to pursue my studies at Edinburgh Napier University. Edinburgh has always been the dream, the end goal to grow old in, the best decision I made was moving here. Moved from having my own flat in Aberdeen, attending college, working hours at the NHS to full time student, living in University halls with 7 complete strangers. As you can imagine the first half of my initial year in Edinburgh was very busy, drinking, meeting new people, meeting my now girlfriend, studies, new city and opportunities to explore. The stereotypical student life isn’t what people always think it is, moving in with 7 complete strangers has its ups and downs, perhaps a story we can explore another time.

When I look back to the person I was back in September 2018, first moving to Edinburgh to the person I am now, it is like two completely different individuals. The nerves I felt about moving were indescribable, but as I said, I doubt I’ll ever make that decision. Many people choose to move away from home to find themselves and as cheesy as this may be, I certainly found who I am supposed to be and where I belong. The nervous 24-year I was when I first moved here, no longer exists two years later. Edinburgh gave me my confidence and for that I shall forever be grateful. I believe making such a large move and decision enabled me to never say no to whatever opportunities came my way, especially within the first year of living here. No task was too large, no jump too scary, another explanation as to why I have met the most amazing, supportive people I get to not only call friends but my Edinburgh family.
